The Tunnel

Yale University Press, 1995.

The Tunnel examines the lives of homeless people who have built homes in an Amtrak tunnel under the West Side Highway, popularly referred to as the "Freedom Tunnel". Consisting of photographs and oral histories Morton collected between 1991 and 1995, the book depicts a variety of improvised accomodations, within old railway cinder-block structures and recesses in the tunnel walls, accessed by tall ladders. Tunnel residents speak to their personal circumstances, articulating the privacy that the Tunnel affords as well as some of the dangers it presents. When Amtrak announced its intention to evict these residents in 1994, Morton and filmmaker Marc Singer worked with Coalition for the Homeless to move tunnel residents into permanent housing.
